2 How to Spot Phishing Emails
  • 🔍
    Check the sender's email address carefully. Phishing emails often use lookalike domains.
    no-reply@microsft-security.com (fake)
    no-reply@microsoft.com (real)
  • ⚠️
    Be suspicious of urgency. "Your account will be deleted in 24 hours" or "Immediate action required" are classic manipulation tactics.
  • 🔗
    Hover over links before clicking. The displayed text may say "microsoft.com" but the actual URL goes somewhere else entirely. On mobile, long-press the link to see the real URL.
  • 👤
    Verify unexpected requests. If your "CEO" emails asking for a wire transfer or your "IT department" wants your password, call them on the phone to verify. Don't reply to the email.
  • 📦
    Don't open unexpected attachments. Especially .exe, .zip, .docm, or .xlsm files. Even PDFs can be malicious.
  • 🎯
    Look for generic greetings. "Dear User" or "Dear Customer" instead of your actual name is a red flag.
  • 💬
    Watch for poor grammar and spelling. While modern phishing is getting better, many attacks still have subtle errors.
3 What to Do If You Suspect Phishing
  • ✅
    DO: Report it to your IT/security team immediately. Most email clients have a "Report Phishing" button.
  • ✅
    DO: Delete the email after reporting it.
  • ✅
    DO: If you already clicked a link or entered credentials, change your password immediately and notify IT.
  • ❌
    DON'T: Click any links or download attachments.
  • ❌
    DON'T: Reply to the email or forward it to coworkers.
  • ❌
    DON'T: Enter your username, password, or any personal information.
