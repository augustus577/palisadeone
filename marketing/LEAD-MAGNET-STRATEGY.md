## THE RESULTS REVIEW CALL (This Is Where You Close)

After they complete the assessment, book a call to review results. Script:

### Opening (2 min)
"Hey [Name], thanks for taking the assessment. Let me walk you through what we found."

### Show Their Score (5 min)
- Overall score out of 100
- Per-framework breakdown (NIST, HIPAA, etc.)
- Highlight RED areas — these are the critical gaps
- "You scored 34% on Access Control — that means right now, there's no guarantee that only authorized people are accessing your systems."

### The Scary Part (3 min)
- "Here's what concerns me about these gaps..."
- Map their gaps to real attack scenarios
- "Without endpoint protection, if one employee clicks a phishing link, an attacker has full access to your network. That's how ransomware starts."

### The Solution (5 min)
- "Here's the good news — every one of these gaps is something we fix."
- Walk through what Palisade One covers
- Show how each gap maps to a feature

### The Close (5 min)
- "Based on what we're seeing, I'd recommend our [Defend] tier. It covers everything flagged in your assessment."
- "$599/month, flat rate, no contracts. We can have you protected by [day]."
- "Want to get started?"

### If They Need to Think
- "Totally understand. I'll send you a summary of your results and our proposal. When would be a good time to follow up — Thursday or Friday?"
- ALWAYS set a specific follow-up time. Never leave it open-ended.

## PRICING OBJECTION HANDLERS

**"We already have antivirus"**
→ "Antivirus is like a lock on your front door. We're the 24/7 security guard watching the cameras, the doors, the windows, and the parking lot. Antivirus catches known threats. We catch everything else."

**"We're too small to be a target"**
→ "43% of cyberattacks target small businesses specifically because they think that. Hackers know you don't have a security team. That's what makes you the easiest target."

**"$399/month is expensive"**
→ "The average ransomware payment for an SMB is $170,000. The average downtime cost is $8,000 per hour. $399/month is insurance that actually prevents the disaster instead of just paying for it after."

**"We need to think about it"**
→ "Absolutely. While you're thinking, I'd recommend at least doing the free assessment so you know where you stand. That way if anything does happen, you'll know what to prioritize. No obligation."

**"Can you do a discount?"**
→ "Our pricing is already aggressive — we're 80% cheaper than comparable MSSPs. But I'll tell you what — sign up this month and I'll throw in a full compliance assessment and policy template package at no extra cost."

**"We have an IT guy"**
→ "That's great — and we're not trying to replace them. We work alongside your IT team. They handle the IT, we handle the security. Your IT person probably doesn't want to be monitoring for threats at 2 AM anyway."

## QUICK WIN: LOCAL NETWORKING

Beyond digital outreach, these close fast:

1. **BNI chapters** (Business Networking International) — Baltimore, Philly, Columbia all have chapters. Show up, give a 60-second pitch, hand out cards.
2. **Local Chamber of Commerce** — Join MD or PA chamber. They have member directories + events.
3. **SCORE / SBA events** — Free small business events where owners gather.
4. **Meetup.com** — Search for business owner meetups in Baltimore/Philly area.
5. **LinkedIn local events** — Attend virtual or in-person business events.

These get you face-to-face with business owners who are already thinking about business problems. Cybersecurity is one of them.
