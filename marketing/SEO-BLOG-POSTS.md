# Cybersecurity for Small Business: The Complete 2026 Guide

If you run a small or mid-sized business, you're a target. Not because you're important to nation-state hackers — but because you're easy.

43% of all cyberattacks target small businesses. The average cost of a breach is $4.88 million. And 60% of small businesses that suffer a major attack close within six months.

This guide covers everything you need to know about protecting your business in 2026 — without needing a dedicated security team or a six-figure budget.

## Why Small Businesses Are the #1 Target

Hackers don't target small businesses because of what they have. They target them because of what they *don't* have:

- No endpoint detection and response (EDR)
- No 24/7 monitoring
- No incident response plan
- No network segmentation
- No employee security training
- Basic antivirus at best

This makes small businesses the path of least resistance. An attacker who would spend weeks trying to breach a Fortune 500 company can compromise an SMB in hours.

## The Top 5 Threats to Small Businesses in 2026

### 1. Ransomware
Ransomware encrypts all your files and demands payment (usually $100K-$500K) to decrypt them. In 2025, ransomware attacks increased 68% year-over-year. Small businesses are now the primary target.

### 2. Business Email Compromise (BEC)
An attacker impersonates your CEO, CFO, or a vendor and tricks an employee into wiring money or sharing sensitive data. BEC cost businesses $2.9 billion in 2024.

### 3. Phishing
Fake emails that look legitimate — from "Microsoft," "your bank," or "a client" — trick employees into clicking malicious links or entering credentials. 91% of all cyberattacks start with a phishing email.

### 4. Insider Threats
Disgruntled employees, careless contractors, or compromised accounts. Not every threat comes from outside.

### 5. Supply Chain Attacks
Hackers compromise a vendor or software provider you trust, then use that access to get into your systems.

## What Every Small Business Needs (Minimum)

1. **Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR)** — Not antivirus. EDR monitors behavior, not just signatures. It catches zero-day threats and responds automatically.

2. **24/7 Monitoring** — Attacks happen at 2 AM on Saturday. If nobody's watching, nobody's responding.

3. **SIEM (Security Information and Event Management)** — Collects and analyzes logs from every device and system. Shows you what's happening across your entire network in real time.

4. **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)** — On everything. Email, VPN, cloud apps, admin accounts. This alone stops 99.9% of credential-based attacks.

5. **Employee Security Training** — Your people are your biggest vulnerability. Train them to recognize phishing.

6. **Incident Response Plan** — What happens when (not if) you get breached? Who do you call? What do you do first?

7. **Compliance Documentation** — If you handle health data (HIPAA), payment cards (PCI), or government contracts (CMMC/NIST), you have legal obligations.
